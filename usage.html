Usage Guidelines#

These guidelines ensure consistent and appropriate use of the Vermont Data Science Group brand across all platforms and materials.

General Principles#

Brand Consistency#

  • Always use official brand assets (logos, colors, fonts)

  • Maintain consistent spacing and layout

  • Follow the guidelines provided in this brand guide

  • When in doubt, ask the communications team

Brand Tone#

The Vermont Data Science Group brand should convey:

  • Professional: Demonstrate expertise and credibility

  • Accessible: Make data science approachable

  • Innovative: Show forward-thinking and creativity

  • Community-focused: Emphasize collaboration and inclusion

Applications#

Digital Media#

Websites#

  • Use the full-color logo in the header

  • Implement brand colors in the UI design

  • Use brand typography for all text elements

  • Maintain consistent spacing and layout

Social Media#

  • Use logo as profile picture (consider icon version for small sizes)

  • Use brand colors in graphics and posts

  • Maintain consistent visual style across platforms

  • Include Vermont Data Science Group branding in shared content

Email#

  • Use logo in email signature

  • Use brand colors for headers and accents

  • Keep design clean and professional

  • Include standard footer with contact information

Print Media#

Business Cards#

  • Feature logo prominently

  • Use brand colors and typography

  • Include essential contact information

  • Maintain adequate white space

Presentations#

  • Use official presentation template (when available)

  • Start with title slide featuring logo

  • Use brand colors for charts and graphics

  • Apply typography guidelines throughout

Reports and Documents#

  • Include logo on cover page

  • Use brand colors for headers and callouts

  • Apply typography guidelines

  • Maintain professional layout

Co-Branding#

When partnering with other organizations:

  • Ensure equal prominence of both brands

  • Maintain adequate separation between logos

  • Do not combine or merge logos

  • Follow both organizations’ brand guidelines

  • Get approval before publishing co-branded materials

Photography and Imagery#

Style#

  • Use high-quality, professional images

  • Prefer authentic photos over stock imagery when possible

  • Images should reflect diversity and inclusion

  • Avoid overly staged or artificial-looking photos

Image Treatment#

  • Maintain natural colors

  • Avoid heavy filters or effects

  • Ensure good composition and lighting

  • Images should complement, not overwhelm, the content

Data Visualization#

  • Follow color palette guidelines

  • Ensure charts are clear and readable

  • Label axes and provide legends

  • Make visualizations accessible (consider colorblind users)

  • Source data and provide context

Approval Process#

Certain materials require approval before publication:

  • New logo variations or adaptations

  • Major marketing campaigns

  • Co-branded materials

  • Significant deviations from brand guidelines

Contact the communications team for approval on questionable uses.

Brand Don’ts#

❌ Do not alter the logo in any way ❌ Do not use unofficial colors or fonts ❌ Do not create new brand elements without approval ❌ Do not use low-quality or pixelated logos ❌ Do not violate clear space requirements ❌ Do not use outdated brand assets

Questions?#

If you have questions about brand usage not covered in this guide, please contact the Vermont Data Science Group communications team.

Thank you for helping maintain our brand integrity!
